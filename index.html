NeoWork Listing QA

Where to Work

Full QA Checklist — In Order

1
Experience TitleTitle Case: connecting words (and, on, in, at, from) must be lowercase. All other words capitalized. 40–70 characters. No "number one experience" — use "highly recommended" or "highly sought" instead.
2
Batch CityVerify city matches the listing. Check the booking URL and description for confirmation.
3
Booking URLMust include utm_source=batch parameter. Open and test the link — confirm it goes to the correct city and correct experience. Critical for multi-location partners.
4
DurationVerify against partner's website or listing description.
5
Starting PriceCheck for accuracy. Partners sometimes list lower pricing on Batch to attract bookings — this is acceptable.
6
LocationMust always be the partner's location — never the customer's. Verify the address matches the partner's actual business.
7
About This ExperienceScan for red flag words: typos, misspellings, duplicate sections. Remove any duplicate "What You'll Do" headers. No need for deep edits unless issues found.
8
Media LibraryCheck all videos and images for external watermarks. Alternate videos and photos — no consecutive videos. Disperse similar-looking photos. Best photo goes first. No blurry content.
9
AttachmentsMenus, maps, schedules, documents only. Disclaimers belong here — never in media. Empty attachments are fine.
10
Ratings and ReviewsMinimum 10 required. If fewer, ask partner to send links or add them directly. Explain why reviews matter.
11
FAQsCopy from partner's website. Skip any FAQ that directly contradicts the listing (e.g., different pricing). One question, one answer per line item.
12
Booking NotesReview if present. Empty is fine.
13
AvailabilityMust be empty for Batch Direct partners. Only populate for email or web partners.
14
Admin TagsAdd relevant experience tags. Most partners have far too few. Use CoStar to generate suggestions.
15
Partner Profile PageCheck for logo, about section, website, Facebook (ID only), Instagram, TikTok, public phone number.
16
Optimize About SectionRun CoStar on the description. Re-optimize title if needed.
17
Send Partner EmailInform partner of optimizations done and list opportunities for further improvement.
18
Mark Complete in TrackerAdd your name. Add a checkmark only after all steps are done.

Media Standards — Detailed Rules

RuleDetail
WatermarksExternal creator watermarks (TikTok, Facebook, YouTube, other accounts): permanently delete OR crop out. Partner's own watermark: acceptable to keep.
ChildrenNever in adult-oriented listings (bachelorette, bachelor parties). In wholesome family experiences: allowed but NEVER in first 4 photos.
Video viewingWatch every video fully — no muting, no skipping. Check for watermarks, inappropriate content, blurry quality throughout.
Video arrangementAlternate video and photo — never 3 consecutive videos. Keeps engagement high.
Similar photosDisperse throughout listing — do not cluster together.
Best photoPlace first. Prefer photos showing people enjoying the experience over product-only shots.
Prohibited in mediaMenus, maps, schedules, documents, disclaimers — these go in Attachments only.

Booking URL — Why UTM Matters

  • Without utm_source=batch: booking recorded as direct traffic — partner gets no credit for Batch referral
  • With utm_source=batch: booking recorded as a Batch referral — partner sees Batch's value
  • If the URL is just a contact page with no UTM, request the partner's proper referral link via Intercom
  • Always open and test the link — multi-location partners may have a Chicago URL that loads Los Angeles by default

Partner Profile — Facebook ID Rule

  • Do NOT paste the full Facebook URL into the partner profile field
  • Copy only the ID portion after facebook.com/ — e.g., if URL is facebook.com/YatsChicago, enter only YatsChicago
  • If the Facebook page shows "content not available," do not add it
  • Public phone number: only add if officially listed as a public contact — never add internal business numbers
QA Email vs Proactive Outreach Email — Key Difference

The QA email focuses on what was optimized AND what opportunities remain (add reviews, videos, social links). This is different from the proactive outreach email from Day 3 which focused on showcasing performance metrics. Both end with the same goal: show the partner Batch is actively working for them.

Pre-Call Preparation

10-Minute Buffer Before Every Call

Pull all data before the call starts. Never search during the call — it makes you sound unprepared and undermines credibility. The partner should feel like you already know their account inside out.

Data to Pull in 10 Minutes

1
How long have they been with Batch?Check Vendor Directory timestamp. Context: 5 years with weak numbers is concerning; 90 days with weak numbers is expected.
2
Subscription tier and price$99 / $150 / $200 / $250 / $300 — determines what discount levers are available
3
Performance metrics (last 90 days)Itinerary adds, favorites, page views, impressions. Translate into layman's terms before the call.
4
Is the listing optimized?Even if yes, can be re-optimized. Check tags, media arrangement, description quality.
5
Is the partner page complete?Logo, about, social links, website. Incomplete = retention offer opportunity.
6
Are they using the UGC collection tool?Check Media Library for "uploaded by user" entries. If not using it — it's already activated for them.
7
Is this a seasonal business?Ask Claude/ChatGPT: "When is peak season for [listing type] in [city]?" Then check historical data for that period.
8
Original reason for joining BatchCheck HubSpot for early communications. Often unavailable — focus on current complaint instead.
9
Preload your solution listWrite down: what's missing on their listing, what you'll offer, possible discounts. Keep it on one document during the call.

UGC Tool Framing

The UGC collection tool is already activated for all partners by default — but most don't know it. Frame it as: "We've activated it for you." This sounds like added value rather than something they already had sitting unused.

Churn Call Framework

Core Principle

You drive the call. The partner is angry. After they rant, it's your turn to present — confidently, comprehensively, without asking for permission after every item. Make them feel heard first, then show up as the expert who has already reviewed their account.

Phase 1

Open the Call

Introduce yourself. Ask if it's a good time. Tell them you've already reviewed their account before calling. Keep this brief — the goal is to transition quickly to letting them speak.

Phase 2

Let Them Rant — Target Their Emotions

Say: "I see you submitted a cancellation request — would you mind filling me in?" Then stop talking. Let them complain fully. After they finish: appreciate their honesty specifically and personally. Do NOT use generic customer service acknowledgment lines.

Phase 3

Assess and Show Value

Present their performance data in layman's terms. Identify their churn type: no bookings, slow ROI, budget, seasonal slowdown, or time/complexity. Frame metrics as proof that things are working — or be honest if the numbers are weak.

Phase 4

Present the Full Optimization Package

Present ALL optimization items at once as one comprehensive package — not one at a time. Show how dedicated you are. Cover: listing optimization, partner page, blog post, UGC tool, ratings/reviews, media. End with: "I'm not going to let you pay for anything additional."

Phase 5

Consolidate and Set Follow-Up

Tell them your team will start immediately and they'll receive an email within 24–48 hours. Schedule a meeting one week before their billing cycle ends to review results together. Ask: "How does that sound?"

Common Churn Reasons and How to Address Each

Churn TypeResponse Strategy
No bookingsExplain that Batch Direct sends traffic, not bookings. Show impressions and page views as proof of visibility. Offer to convert to email/bookable partner so they can see actual booking ROI.
Slow ROISEO takes 3 months to compound. Show where they are in the timeline. If past 3 months with zero results, be honest — offer optimization as last attempt.
Budget concernsSave discount offers for after optimization pitch. First show value, then offer 25% off or $99/month.
Seasonal slowdownIdentify their peak season. Explain that maintaining the listing now builds the audience needed 3 months before peak season starts.

Scripts & Key Phrases

Opening the Call
"Hey [Name], this is Rick from Batch. How are you doing today? ... Great. So I'm reaching out because I see you submitted a cancellation request, and I wanted to personally connect with you. I've actually reviewed your account before this call. Is this a good time to chat? ... Perfect. Would you mind filling me in on what's been going on?"
Then stop. Let them talk. Don't interrupt.
After They Rant — Emotional Target
"First of all, I really appreciate you being this honest with me. I genuinely needed to hear this — the specific things that are affecting your decision. That's exactly the kind of feedback that helps us do better for you."
Do NOT say: "Thank you for letting me know." Generic = dismissed.
Presenting Metrics
"So I was looking at your account before this call and here's what I see. In the last 90 days, your listing showed up [X] times in search results — that's your listing appearing every single day in front of customers actively looking for experiences in your area. On top of that, [X] real Batch users clicked your listing and reviewed it. Those are people who stopped, looked, and considered your experience."
Presenting the Optimization Package
"Here's what I want to do for you. I've already looked at your listing and I see a few things that, if we fix them now, will make a real difference. I'm going to re-optimize your listing with better keywords and descriptions so you show up higher in search. We've also created a partner page for you on Batch — that's a second page working alongside your listing to pull in even more visibility. And I want to create a blog post for you — completely free — that gives you a third page pointing customers toward your experience. I'm going to do all of this for you, and I'm not going to let you pay for anything additional. This is on us."
Present all items together — don't pause and ask "how does that sound" after each one.
Consolidating and Setting the Follow-Up
"My team is going to get started on all of this right away. Expect an email from us within the next 24 to 48 hours — we'll walk you through everything we've done and what we still need from you, because we want to work side by side with you on this. And then I'd love to schedule a meeting with you one week before your billing date so we can look at the numbers together and see what improved. How does that sound?"
Ending a Cancelled Call Positively
"I've gone ahead and cancelled your subscription in our system. Your listing will stay active until [billing end date] at no additional charge, and then it will be deactivated. I really appreciate your time today, [Name], and I genuinely wish you all the best."
Do NOT say "Is there anything else I can help you with?" — you didn't help them. End warmly and directly.

Do Say

  • "I'm not going to let you pay for anything additional."
  • "Would you mind filling me in?"
  • "I appreciate how honest you've been with me."
  • "Your listing showed up X times in search results."
  • "How does that sound?"
  • "My team will start immediately."

Never Say

  • "This is free." (say "at no additional cost" instead)
  • "Partners on your tier typically see..." (comparisons hurt)
  • "That's strange, most partners do well." (invalidating)
  • "What you're seeing is normal for this time of year." (dismissive)
  • "That's fair, let me put it in context for you." (condescending)
  • "Is there anything else I can help you with?" (after a cancellation)
  • "I totally hear that and want to figure out why together." (passive)

Retention Levers — Updated Sequence

Key Rule: Never Offer Levers One at a Time

Presenting one item, then asking "how does that sound?" — then the next item — gives partners repeated chances to say no. Present the full optimization package as one bundle first. Only move to pricing levers if they refuse the package.

The Full Lever Sequence

LeverWhat It IsWhen to OfferWho Delivers
Lever 1 Full optimization package (listing, partner page, blog post, UGC, ratings, media) Always — first and primary offer Rick on the call
Lever 2 Switch from quarterly to monthly subscription billing If partner is concerned about upfront cost — reduces commitment burden Rick on the call
Lever 3 25% discount for 2 months, then $150/month, then $99/month After Levers 1 and 2 are refused — escalate pricing offers in this order Rick on the call
Lever 4 Email/bookable partner conversion — see actual booking ROI inside Batch Final offer — can combine with $99/month to close Rick on the call

Optimization Package Contents

  • Listing re-optimization — keywords, title, description (CoStar)
  • Partner page completion — logo, about, social links, website
  • Blog post — free, creates a 3rd indexed page for visibility
  • UGC collection tool activation — "We've activated it for you"
  • Ratings and reviews upload — ask partner to send Google review link
  • Media optimization — arrangement, alternating, best photo first

Present all of these together as one comprehensive package. Never list them one by one and ask for approval each time.

Why Email/Bookable Conversion is the Cream on Top

Batch Direct partners can't see which bookings came from Batch — so they feel they're getting nothing. Converting to email/bookable lets them see actual booking records inside Batch. This solves the root problem: transparent ROI. Saved for last because once they see real bookings, the value becomes undeniable.

Discount Timing Strategy

  • Lever 3 order: 25% off for 2 months first, then offer $150/month, then $99/month — escalate only if refused
  • Ideal timing: Offer discounts at the 7-day-before-billing-end meeting, after showing improved metrics
  • Why wait: By then you have data showing optimization worked — the discount reinforces staying, not replacing value
  • If they refuse optimization immediately (70% of cases): Move through Levers 2, 3, and 4 on the first call
  • No more free months: Eliminated — creates extra work for partners who churn anyway

Seasonal Partners

The 3+3 Month Rule

SEO takes 3 months to compound. For customers to book during peak season, they plan 1–3 months ahead. So the partner needs to be live and optimized 3–6 months before their peak season to build an audience in time.

How to Identify and Handle Seasonal Partners

1
Identify peak seasonAsk the partner directly, OR use Claude/ChatGPT: "When is peak season for [listing type] in [city] in the US?"
2
Check historical dataFilter performance metrics to their peak season months. If peak season shows zero results, the partner likely should churn — don't make false promises.
3
Frame the current period correctlyIf they're in off-season: "Right now is actually the best time to build your audience for peak season in [month]. The listings that rank highest in June are the ones that were active and optimized in March."
4
Offer lower pricing to keep listing aliveDuring off-season: offer $99/month to maintain the listing. Explain that canceling now means starting from scratch when peak season returns.
5
Recommend continuous media additionsKeep adding pictures/videos during off-season. The UGC tool helps collect these from customers automatically.
⚠️ Never Say "What You're Seeing Is Normal"

This sounds dismissive and will make the partner angrier. Instead frame it proactively: "This type of listing peaks in [month]. Right now is actually the critical window to build the audience that will convert bookings when your season starts." The message is the same — but you're giving them a reason to stay, not excusing poor results.

Cancellation Process

When to Cancel

Only proceed to cancellation if the partner refuses all optimization offers AND all retention levers. Do not keep pitching after they've said no to everything — proceed to Stripe immediately and end the call professionally.

Stripe Cancellation — Step by Step

1
Tell the partner"I understand. Let me go ahead and cancel your subscription now. Please bear with me for just a moment."
2
Open StripeFind the partner's subscription. Click the three dots next to "Update Subscription."
3
Select "Cancel Subscription"Choose "End of Current Period" — not immediate. This keeps the listing active through the paid period.
4
Confirm cancellationNote the billing end date (e.g., paid April 26, ends May 26).
5
Inform the partner"Your subscription has been cancelled. Your listing will stay active until [date] at no additional charge. After that, it will be fully deactivated and you won't be billed again."
6
End the call warmly"I really appreciate your time today, [Name], and I wish you all the best." Click end. Do not ask if there's anything else you can help with.

What NOT to Do After Cancellation is Confirmed

  • Do NOT continue doing optimization work for a confirmed churning partner — wasted effort per Stefan's direction
  • Do NOT ask "Is there anything else I can help you with?" — you didn't help them keep their listing
  • Do NOT offer more discounts after they've confirmed — respect their decision
  • Do NOT cancel immediately — always use "end of current period" to give them the time they paid for

Two-Call Model for Churn

CallTimingPurposeLevers Available
Call 1 When churn request comes in Acknowledge, show value, present full optimization package Lever 1 (optimization) + Lever 2 (monthly billing)
Call 2 7 days before billing end date Review improved metrics, make final offer, or confirm cancellation Levers 3 (25% off, $150, $99) + Lever 4 (email/bookable)

If partner refuses optimization on Call 1 (70% of cases): skip to pricing levers immediately on the same call. No need to wait for Call 2.
