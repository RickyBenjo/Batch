Churn Case Study — Main Street Motion

What This Case Teaches

This is a full real-world walkthrough of how to handle a cancellation request — from investigation to optimization to email strategy. Study every step.

Case Background

  • Partner requested cancellation, claims no one responded via Intercom
  • Cliff checked Intercom, HubSpot, and Airtable — no conversation history found
  • Contact email (way.mainstreetshop.com) yielded no results in any system
  • Conclusion: partner likely being dishonest about lack of response — no notes = no searchable record
  • Partner explicitly refused a 30-minute consultation call and demanded immediate cancellation
  • Since they won't engage by phone: proceed with cancellation attempt while still showing value received

⚠️ Tags Are Critical — Most Partners Have Too Few

Tags function as backend keywords. Customers search terms that may not appear anywhere in your listing text — tags capture those searches. Always compare your partner's tags to competitors. A well-optimized competitor might have 20+ tags vs your partner's 2–3.

  • Do NOT add tags marked only for Boost or Party Pro features
  • Use CoStar to generate relevant tag suggestions
  • Tags ≠ description keywords — they work in addition to, not instead of, description SEO

Email Communication Strategy

Golden Rule — Acknowledge First

Always open by acknowledging the partner's request before anything else. Partners need to feel heard. Lead with "We understand you'd like to cancel..." — not with metrics or company actions. This was the key correction on the Main Street Motion draft.

Two-Email Strategy (Same Intercom Thread)

Never send one overwhelming email. Use two touchpoints in the same thread:

EmailContentSubject/Header
Email 1 1. Acknowledge the cancellation request
2. Explain constraints (3-month term, no refund)
3. Show performance metrics (impressions + page views only)
4. Offer to optimize/boost listing instead 		New subject line for the thread
Email 2 Specific details of optimizations performed:
— Media rearrangement
— Tags added
— Content re-optimized
— Blog feature offer
— Request for partner actions (add reviews, videos) 		Header within the same thread body (not a new subject)

This phased approach prevents partners from seeing it as spam and gives them time to digest the first email before receiving detailed optimization specifics.

Email Tone — Plain Language Rules

  • 90% of core partners are small business owners — no corporate jargon
  • Instead of "incentivize customers" → say "customers have a chance to win a surprise"
  • Instead of "SEO optimization" → say "we added keywords so more customers find you when they search"
  • Treat partners like customers — conversational language that works in phone calls too
  • Avoid M-dashes in AI-generated content — they signal artificial generation
  • Always review AI output before sending — never send a template verbatim

Updated Retention Policy

⚠️ Policy Change — Free Months Are Eliminated

Effective Day 3, the team will no longer offer free months or 30-day pauses. These were found to be ineffective and create extra work for partners who churn anyway.

New Final Option — Convert to Email/Bookable Partner

Instead of a free month, offer to convert the partner to an email or bookable listing. This lets partners see actual bookings and payments directly in their Batch account — solving the core problem: partners can't track which bookings came from Batch. With bookable listings, they get transparent booking and payment records, making Batch's ROI demonstrable and measurable.

Timing Strategy for Retention Offers

Time RemainingAction
Less than 3 weeksSimply confirm the cancellation — no retention attempt
1 month+Offer optimization services + schedule follow-up call to show improved metrics
1 week before end dateProactively reach out with pricing retention offers (25% off, $99, $150)

Why the Email/Bookable Conversion Solves the Core Problem

  • Many partners don't see ROI from direct website traffic — they can't track which bookings came from Batch
  • With email/bookable listings: partners log in to accept/decline bookings or offer alternate times
  • They see actual booking records and payments inside Batch
  • This makes value tangible — not just traffic numbers, but real bookings they can see
  • A new SOP for the email/bookable conversion process needs to be created
