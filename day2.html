Five Booking Methods

Why This Matters

Each booking method requires a completely different support approach. Knowing the method upfront determines how much intervention is needed and who handles what.

Method How Bookings Work Rick's Role Intervention Level
Batch Direct External redirect link. Customer sent to partner's own website. No booking placed on Batch at all. Primary focus. Show page views, favorites, itinerary adds as value proof. Low — no booking management
Email Partner Customer places booking on Batch. Partner receives it in their portal and can confirm, decline, or offer alternate times. Monitor; assist if partner doesn't respond within 7 days. Medium — partner actions in portal
Web Partner Customer places booking on Batch. Partner cannot action it themselves. Batch support must go to partner's website and place the booking manually. Must visit partner website + place booking + mark confirmed in system. High — manual placement required
Fair Harbor API Automatic booking integration. Minimal intervention needed. Refunds go to Batch first and must be manually processed through Fair Harbor's system. Escalate setup requests to onshore team. Handle refund routing when needed. High for setup/refunds — escalate
PEAK API Similar to Fair Harbor — automatic booking integration. Minimal daily intervention. Escalate setup requests onshore. Monitor for issues. Medium — mostly automated

When a Fair Harbor partner cancels and refunds a customer, the refund goes to Batch first, not directly to the customer. The team must manually process it through Fair Harbor's system. Batch Direct Partner — Key Distinction

In the Batch admin, Batch Direct partner listings only show three tabs:

  • Listing details — content, media, descriptions
  • Edit history — change log
  • Admin tab — backend settings

No booking calendar or order management — because all bookings happen on the partner's own site. Also: Batch Direct partners want to see revenue metrics, not just itinerary add data.

Listing Health Framework

Three-Part Health Assessment

Every listing evaluation covers three areas in this order: Listing Details → Partner Profile → Performance Metrics. Together they tell you what's working, what needs improvement, and what to offer the partner.

Part 1: Listing Details

  • Title (catchy + SEO-optimized)
  • Description
  • Pricing
  • Location
  • Duration
  • Booking options
  • FAQs
  • Booking notes
  • Availability info
  • Media (photos + video)

Part 2: Partner Profile

  • Logo
  • About section (copy from listing)
  • Website link
  • Public phone number
  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • TikTok

Profile is a new page that boosts SEO and traction. Fill it fully.

Part 3: Performance Metrics

  • Page views
  • Favorites
  • Direct link clicks
  • Itinerary adds ← strongest signal

Itinerary adds = customer has selected listing and plans to book within 30–90 days.

  • Bars — different visual presentation, tailored approach needed

CoStar prompt currently only covers regular experiences. Other types need their own optimization prompts — opportunity for future development.

Communication Strategy — Speak Plain English

Partners are small business owners. Avoid jargon like "visibility" or "SEO optimization." Instead say: "Customers want to see other customers enjoying the experience, so adding videos helps them imagine themselves enjoying it too." Build rapport, educate simply, make offers — then do the work after the call via email.

Handling Already-Optimized Listings

Don't tell a partner "your listing isn't optimized." Even if it looks good, you can offer value:

  • "We can try to re-optimize your listing to boost it more."
  • "We can try to add more keywords to help you show up more when customers search."

This shows the partner Batch is actively working for them, even when the listing is already solid.

Partner Tenure Context

Always check how long a partner has been with Batch using the Vendor Directory timestamp. Context changes everything:

  • 5 itinerary adds/month might be strong for a new partner
  • 5 itinerary adds/month for a 5-year partner is concerning — performance may be stagnant or declining

Example from training: Alikat Adventure — 5-year partner, only modest monthly metrics. Requires different framing than a new partner with the same numbers.

CoStar SEO Optimization Tool

What is CoStar?

A reusable AI prompt created by Cliff (with Felix, Ervin, and Kring) that allows any team member to optimize listings without premium AI accounts. Works on free tiers of Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT — rotate between them when limits are hit.

Five-Task Optimization Workflow

1
Search for SEO keywordsSpecific to the listing type, city, and experience category
2
Optimize the titleIncorporate primary keywords while keeping it catchy
3
Optimize the descriptionRewrite considering keywords — adds "What you'll do" and "Why we love it for your party" sections
4
Generate partner emailSummarizes all changes made — ready to send to partner
5
Update partner profile pageAdd keyword-rich content to the partner's profile for additional search visibility

How to Use the CoStar Form

Fill in this form, paste into any free AI tool, and receive optimized outputs:

Time Savings

  • Manual optimization: 20–30 minutes per listing
  • With CoStar: ~5 minutes per listing
  • Irvin and Felix already using it daily

SEO Timeline Expectations

  • Compounding effect — gets stronger over time

The Email Outreach tool and CoStar prompt can generate inaccurate claims. Example: the template may say "revenue continuing to flow" when a partner has had zero bookings. Read every output carefully before sending to a partner. Fix anything that doesn't match reality.

Four-Lever Retention Strategy

Key Principle — Don't Give Everything at Once

Offering all four levers in a single call makes the company sound desperate. The strategy uses Ervene and Felix to deliver early-level offers via Intercom before Rick calls — so Rick only needs to offer the final tiers, positioning him as someone with real authority.

L1

Level 1 — Price Reduction

Reduce from standard pricing to ~$150/month. Offered first via Intercom by Ervene/Felix before the call.

L2

Level 2 — Further Reduction to $99

Drop to the floor price of $99/month. Still offered via Intercom before Rick's call.

L3

Level 3 — One Month Free

Pause subscription in Stripe for one free month. Rick handles this on the call. Implemented ~one month ago — still gathering data on effectiveness.

L4

Level 4 — Additional Incentives

Blog post, listing optimization, keyword additions, or other high-value add-ons. Rick's final play on the call.

⚠️ Free Month Exploitation Risk

Some partners may take the free month and cancel anyway — costing Batch money with no retention benefit. Mitigation: use the free month to complete optimizations so partners see measurable improvement. Track which partners converted vs churned after the free period to gather data.

Airtable Workflows & Task Management

Airtable as Your Documentation Safety Net

  • Log all activities, decisions, and communications in Airtable
  • Serves as an audit trail to protect yourself if blame is shifted
  • Add Intercom conversations, Slack messages, and notes as comments on partner records
  • If you make a mistake: fix it transparently, make the correction visible to everyone — don't hide it
  • Overcommunicate and overdocument as a default practice
